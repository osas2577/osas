Family planning and cervical cancer

Cervical cancer results from uncontrolled, untreated growth of abnormal cells in the cervix. Human papillomavirus (HPV), an STI, can cause abnormal cells on the cervix to develop and grow. HPV is found on skin in the genital area and also in the tissues of the vagina, cervix, and mouth. It is primarily transmitted through skin-to-skin contact. Vaginal, anal, and oral sex can also spread HPV, as can digital contact. Prioritize Your Health, Secure Your Future: Discover Comprehensive Family Planning and Life-Saving Cervical Cancer Check-ups at Obio Cottage Hospital. Invest in Your Well-being Today.
